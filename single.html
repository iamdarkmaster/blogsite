Blancos Youth Ecosystem in Mitigating Climate Change

The global climate conversation often orbits around carbon emissions, deforestation, and fossil fuels. Yet, a quieter but equally powerful culprit continues to shape the fate of our ecosystems plastic waste.

Plastics contribute to climate change at every stage of their life cycle, from the extraction of raw materials to manufacturing, transportation, and eventual disposal. It is in this broader reality that the efforts of Blancos Youth Ecosystem have emerged, not just as a recycling venture but as a climate action movement rooted in local experience and global relevance.

The production of plastics is deeply intertwined with fossil fuels. The majority of plastic products are derived from natural gas and crude oil, and the refining process is energy-intensive. According to the Center for International Environmental Law (2019), plastic production could account for up to 13% of the total global carbon budget by 2050 if left unchecked. But the story doesn't end there.

Once plastics are used and discarded, they continue to emit greenhouse gases. As they break down especially under sunlight, plastics release methane and ethylene, gases that exacerbate global warming. The situation worsens in aquatic environments, where microplastics degrade slowly but continuously pollute and warm sensitive ecosystems.

Along the banks of the Techno River, the consequences of plastic pollution have long been visible blocked water channels, dying aquatic life, and the decay of unmanaged waste. Blancos Youth Ecosystem, in launching the Techno River Cleanup, recognized that river restoration is more than aesthetics and it is climate repair.

By removing polypropylene and polyethylene waste from the river, the organization reduces the environmental load of plastics that would otherwise degrade into methane and ethylene. The cleanup also prevents these plastics from reaching larger water bodies, thereby safeguarding aquatic biodiversity and stabilizing delicate microclimates within riparian ecosystems.
